The Challenge

When Snapchat went public in Spring 2017, NPR turned to Youth Radio to help listeners understand the huge appeal of the app, which teens understand intuitively, while older people can--let’s be honest--be completely clueless about how it even works.

Whether you love Snapchat or hate it, use this tutorial to make your bootleg version. Your app will let users take a photo, draw on it, add things to it, and send it to your friends. We can’t wait to see what you come up with from there!

Check out Youth Radio’s Snapchat story for NPR here.

Snapchat Remix Tutorial

1. Introduction

Let's make an app that can take a photo and set it as the background, and draw on the image.

This is the initial screen you will see in the viewer. The components include a label, a button, a canvas, and a camera.

2. Add Button click event

Click on the "Blocks" button in the upper right corner.

Click pictureButton and drag when pictureButton.Click to the Viewer.

3. Take a photo on click

Click on the Camera1 drawer and drag out call Camera1.TakePicture and add it to when pictureButton.Click.

Try out your app! See if your app takes a picture when you press the camera button.

4. Begin to set the Canvas background to the image.

Click on Camera1 again and drag out the when Camera1.AfterPicture block to the Viewer.

5. Set the background image.

Now, click Canvas1 in Blocks and drag out set Canvas1.BackgroundImage to and add it to when Camera1.AfterPicture.

6. Set background image using the image you just took.

Hover over "image" on the when Camera1.AfterPicture block and click on get image. Connect it to set Canvas1.BackgroundImage to.

7. Add when Canvas1.Dragged.

Click on Canvas1 and add when Canvas1.Dragged to the Viewer.

8. Add call Canvas1.DrawLine.

Click on Canvas1 again and add the call Canvas1.DrawLine to the when Canvas1.Dragged block.

This procedure tells the app where each line starts and ends.

9. Add get prevX to "x1"

Hover over startX on call Canvas1.DrawLine and click on get prevX. Add it to the "x1" socket of call Canvas1.DrawLine.

10. Add get prevY to "y1"

Repeat with get prevY.

11. Add get currentX to "x2"

And get currentX.

12. Add get currenty to "y2"

And finally, get currenty.

13. Test your app!

Does your app take a picture? Can you set the picture to the canvas background? Can you draw on your image with your finger?

If so, move on to the next section where you can add expansions!

Published apps

Hello World

Shake to clear your image

In Designer, click on Sensors and drag and drop an AccelerometerSensor onto the Viewer.

Click Blocks.

Click on AccelerometerSensor1 and drag out when AccelerometerSensor1.Shaking and add it under your existing blocks.

Then click on Canvas1 and connect the call Canvas1.clear block.

Test out your app! After drawing on your image, shake the canvas and watch your drawing disappear!

Draw dots on the canvas

Click Blocks.

Click on Canvas1 and choose the when Canvas1.Touched block.

Click on Canvas1 again and choose the call Canvas1.DrawCircle block and add it to when Canvas1.Touched.

Hover over the red "x" button and choose the get x block and drag it to the centerX socket of call Canvas1.DrawCircle.

Repeat with the get y block.

Click on the Math drawer and drag out the 0 block. Connect it to the radius socket of call Canvas1.DrawCircle and change the number to 5 (or any other number).

Test out your app! Tap on your canvas to draw dots!

Pick image from device

In Designer, click Media and drag an ImagePicker into your Viewer.

Click Blocks.

Click on ImagePicker1, and select when ImagePicker.AfterPicking.

Click on Canvas1, and connect the set Canvas1.BackgroundImage to block.

Click on ImagePicker1 and connect the ImagePicker1.Selection block.

Test out your app! Choose an image from your photo roll and set it as your background.

Change color of paint

In Designer, add a Button to your Viewer.

Change the text of your button so the user knows which color they're selecting. You can also change the size, color, and shape of the buttons.

Click Blocks.

Click Button1 and drag out the when Button1.Click block.

Click on Canvas1 and choose set Canvas1.PaintColor to and connect it.

Click on Colors and choose a blue block and connect it to set Canvas1.PaintColor to.

Test out your app! Once it works, add more buttons to give the user more options for colors.

Random color button

In Designer, add a Button to your Viewer.

Change the text of your button so the user knows which color they're selecting. You can also change the size, color, and shape of the buttons.

Click Blocks.

Click on Button1 and drag out when Button1.Click.

Click Canvas1 and connect set Canvas1.PaintColor to.

The random colors will be selected from a list of colors. Click Lists and select pick a random item list.

Click Lists again and drag out make a list.

Click on the blue gear and click and drag as many items to your list as you want.

Click on Colors and drag out a color block to fill an empty socket.

Repeat with the rest of the sockets.

Test out your app! Once it works, you can add more colors to the list.

Add text to canvas

In Designer, go to User Interface and add a Button to your Viewer. Change the text of the button in the Properties panel if you want.

Again in User Interface, add a TextBox to your Viewer.

Click Blocks.

Click on Button1 and drag out when Button1.Click.

Click on Canvas 1 next and connect call Canvas1.DrawText.

Click TextBox1 and add "TextBox1.Text" to the "text" socket of the call Canvas1.DrawText block.

Next, click on Math and drag out two 0 blocks and connect them to the x and y sockets.

Change the values of both the 0 blocks to 10 (or anywhere else you want the blocks to be).

Test out your app! Once it works, you can move around the text or let the user control where the text goes.

Add a share button

In Designer, go to User Interface and add a Button to your Viewer. Change the text of the button in the Properties panel if you want.

Click Social and add Sharing to your Viewer.

Click Blocks.

Click on Button1 and drag out when Button1.Click.

Then click on Sharing1 and connect the call Sharing1.ShareFileWithMessage block.

Next, click on Canvas1 and connect call Canvas1.SaveAs to the "file" socket of call Sharing1.ShareFileWithMessage.

Click on Text and add a " " block to the "fileName" socket of call Canvas1.SaveAs. Change the text inside to "myPic" or anything else you want to name your file.

Click on Text and and add a " " block to the "message socket" of call Sharing1.ShareFileWithMessage. Change the text inside to "Look at my picture!" or anything else you want to name your file.

Test out your app! Once it works, you can try out sending pictures to your friends! This component works similarly to other sharing components so try them as well!

Share picture to Twitter

In Designer, go to User Interface and add a Button to your Viewer. Change the text of the button in the Properties panel if you want.

Click Social and add Sharing to your Viewer.

Follow this link in order to set up your Consumer Key and Consumer Secret. Start by clicking on Create New App.

Fill in the form to create your app.

When your application has been created, click on the Keys and Access Tokens tab.

Copy the Consumer Key and the Consumer Secret.

Back in Designer, click on Twitter1 and in Properties, add your created Consumer Key and Consumer Secret to the corresponding boxes.

Click Blocks.

Click on Button1 and drag out when Button1.Click.

Click on Twitter1 and connect the call Twitter1.Authorize block.

Click on Twitter1 again and drag out when Twitter1.IsAuthorized.

Click on Twitter1 again and connect call Twitter1.TweetWithImage to when Twitter1.IsAuthorized.

Click on Text and grab a " " and connect it to the "status" socket of call Twitter1.TweetWithImage. Change your text to "Look at my picture!" or anything else you'd like.

Click Canvas1 and connect call Canvas1.SaveAs to the ImagePath socket of call Twitter1.TweetWithImage.

Click on Text and grab a " " and connect it to the "status" socket of call Twitter1.TweetWithImage. Change your text to "Look at my picture!" or anything else you'd like.

Test out your app! Once it works, you can tweet images and try out some of the functions available with the Twitter component!
